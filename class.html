The Graphic Design and Photography course introduces students to design theory, digital illustration, modeling, prototyping, and photography. Students will explore 2D and 3D art forms while learning the importance of graphic design.

  • Unit 2: Students will develop and apply design skills using software to create visual designs.
  • Unit 3: Students will use vector and photo editing software to design and prototype creative solutions.
  • Unit 4: Students will learn photo editing techniques, use DSLR camera settings (ISO, shutter speed, F-stop), and apply composition principles to create and enhance photographs.
  • By the end of the course, students will create a professional portfolio showcasing their designs, images, and prototypes using industry-standard tools and software.

Students use programs like Adobe Illustrator and Adobe Photoshop. Adobe Illustrator is a professional vector graphics editor used for creating scalable designs like logos, illustrations, and graphics for print and digital media. Adobe Photoshop is a powerful image editing and graphic design software used for creating, enhancing, and manipulating photos, digital art, and visual content.

What Grades Normally Take This Class?

Students in grades 9-12 take this class. After completing Graphic Design and Photography 1, students can choose to take Digital Media 2.

What careers are related to Graphic Design?

Students can become graphic designers, web designers or illustrators. Students can major in graphic design in college.
